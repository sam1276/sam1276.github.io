Question 1

Section A 1 Marks

State True or False:
“In a Python program, if a break statement is given in a nested loop, it terminates the execution of all loops in one go.”

Answer
False.
Explanation

In Python, if a `break` statement is given in a nested loop (a loop inside another loop), it will only terminate the innermost loop in which the `break` statement is placed. The outer loop(s) will continue their execution as usual.

Question 2

Section A 1 Marks

In a table in MYSQL database, an attribute A of datatype varchar(20) has the value “Keshav”. The attribute B of datatype char(20) has value “Meenakshi”. How many characters are occupied by attribute A and attribute B?

a. 20,6

b. 6,20

c. 9,6

d. 6,9

Answer
c. 9,6
Explanation

Attribute A (VARCHAR): Stores "Keshav" (6 characters).
Attribute B (CHAR): Stores "Meenakshi" (20 characters, padded with spaces).
Hence, the correct option is: c. 9,6

Question 3

Section A 1 Marks

What will be the output of the following statement:
print(3-2**2**3+99/11)

a. 244

b. 244.0

c. -244.0

d. Error

Answer
c. -244.0
Explanation

To calculate the output of the given statement, follow the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS):

Exponentiation: 2**3 evaluates to 8.
Exponentiation: 2**8 evaluates to 256.
Subtraction: 3 - 256 evaluates to -253.
Division: 99/11 evaluates to 9.
Addition: -253 + 9 evaluates to -244.

So, the output of the statement print(3-2**2**3+99/11) is -244.

Question 4

Section A 1 Marks

Select the correct output of the code:

s="Python is fun"
l = s.split()
s_new = "-".join([l[0].upper(), l[1], l[2].capitalize()])
print (s_new)

a. PYTHON-IS-Fun

b. PYTHON-is-Fun

c. Python-is-fun

d. PYTHON-Is -Fun

Answer
b. "PYTHON-is-Fun"
Explanation

`s` is the given string: "Python is fun".
`l = s.split()` splits the string `s` into a list of words using the default space as the separator, and the resulting list `l` becomes `["Python", "is", "fun"]`.
`l[0].upper()` takes the first word from the list `l`, which is "Python", and uses the `upper()` method to convert it to uppercase, resulting in "PYTHON".
`l[1]` takes the second word from the list `l`, which is "is".
`l[2].capitalize()` takes the third word from the list `l`, which is "fun", and uses the `capitalize()` method to capitalize the first letter, resulting in "Fun".

Finally, the `join()` method is used to concatenate the modified elements of the list `l` using a hyphen (`-`) as the separator. The resulting string `s_new` will be:

"PYTHON-is-Fun"

Question 5

Section A 1 Marks

In MYSQL database, if a table, Alpha has degree 5 and cardinality 3, and another table, Beta has degree 3 and cardinality 5, what will be the degree and cardinality of the Cartesian product of Alpha and Beta?

a. 5,3

b. 8,15

c. 3,5

d. 15,8

Answer
b. 8,15
Explanation

The degree of the Cartesian product of two tables is the sum of the degrees of the original tables. In this case, the degree will be 5 + 3 = 8.

The cardinality of the Cartesian product of two tables is the product of the cardinalities of the original tables. In this case, the cardinality will be 3 * 5 = 15.

So, the degree of the Cartesian product is 8, and the cardinality is 15.
